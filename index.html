About Biratnagar

Biratnagar is Nepal’s second-largest city and a major industrial hub. Located near the Indian border, it is known for its vibrant culture, bustling markets, and diverse communities.

Attractions

An important religious site attracting many pilgrims.

A lively market with local goods, textiles, and street food.

A nearby historic town with cultural and historical significance.

Food in Biratnagar

Biratnagar offers a variety of cuisines from traditional Nepali dishes like momo and dal-bhat to Indian-inspired street food like samosas and chaat.

Transport

Biratnagar is well connected by road and air. Rickshaws, buses, and taxis are the main forms of local transport, and Biratnagar Airport connects the city to Kathmandu.

Biratnagar, Nepal

Did you know? Biratnagar is the birthplace of several of Nepal’s political leaders and has played a major role in the country’s history.
